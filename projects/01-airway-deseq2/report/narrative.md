## Executive Summary

Differential expression analysis was performed on RNA-sequencing data from the *airway* dataset (Himes et al., 2014; PMID 24926665), comparing dexamethasone-treated (1 µM, 18 hours) versus untreated primary human airway smooth muscle cells across four paired cell lines. Of 16,596 genes subjected to statistical testing, 4,099 (24.7%) were identified as differentially expressed at a false discovery rate-adjusted significance threshold (padj < 0.05), comprising 2,201 upregulated and 1,898 downregulated genes in the treated condition relative to untreated controls. Principal component analysis revealed that the first two principal components accounted for 48% and 24% of total variance, respectively, suggesting that treatment condition represents the dominant source of transcriptional variation in this dataset, with a secondary source of structured variance likely reflecting inter-individual differences among the four cell lines. The magnitude and breadth of the transcriptional response are consistent with the well-characterized pleiotropic activity of glucocorticoid receptor signaling in airway smooth muscle cells.

## Results Interpretation

The five most statistically significant differentially expressed genes exhibited log2 fold changes ranging from approximately 2.95 to 4.57, corresponding to roughly 8- to 24-fold differences in estimated expression between treated and untreated conditions, all with Benjamini-Hochberg adjusted p-values below 4 × 10⁻¹¹⁹. Among these, *DUSP1* (ENSG00000120129; log2FC = 2.95, padj = 1.22 × 10⁻¹²⁴, baseMean = 3412.97) is a well-established glucocorticoid-responsive gene encoding dual specificity phosphatase 1, a negative regulator of mitogen-activated protein kinase pathways; its upregulation in this context is consistent with prior literature and lends biological plausibility to the observed transcriptional profile. *SPARCL1* (ENSG00000152583; log2FC = 4.57, padj = 3.58 × 10⁻¹³²), encoding a secreted extracellular matrix-associated protein, and *CACNB2* (ENSG00000165995; log2FC = 3.29, padj = 8.60 × 10⁻¹³²), encoding a voltage-gated calcium channel auxiliary subunit, exhibited the strongest statistical evidence for differential expression, though the functional significance of their association with dexamethasone treatment in this cell type requires further experimental investigation beyond the scope of this observational analysis. *SAMHD1* (ENSG00000101347; log2FC = 3.76, padj = 1.88 × 10⁻¹²⁴, baseMean = 12718.31) and *MAOA* (ENSG00000189221; log2FC = 3.35, padj = 2.57 × 10⁻¹¹⁹) also ranked among the most significantly associated genes; notably, *SAMHD1* displayed the highest baseMean of the five, indicating robust baseline expression across samples. It should be emphasized that large effect sizes alone, in the absence of functional validation, are insufficient to establish biological importance, and the genes highlighted here are prioritized on statistical grounds rather than confirmed mechanistic relevance.

## Limitations

Several limitations inherent to the experimental design and analytical approach should be considered when interpreting these findings. The dataset comprises only four cell lines, each measured under both conditions in a paired design, which, while statistically efficient for controlling inter-individual variability, constrains the generalizability of results to broader populations of human airway smooth muscle cells and does not permit inference regarding patient-level heterogeneity in glucocorticoid responsiveness. Although the paired design was accounted for in the differential expression model, the small sample size may reduce the reliability of dispersion estimates for lowly expressed genes, and residual batch or cell line-specific effects cannot be entirely excluded as contributors to the observed transcriptional differences. Furthermore, as with all observational transcriptomic studies, the associations reported here are correlative in nature; the identification of a gene as differentially expressed following dexamethasone exposure does not establish a direct regulatory relationship, and downstream functional validation—such as chromatin immunoprecipitation, reporter assays, or loss-of-function experiments—would be required to substantiate mechanistic claims. Finally, the analysis was conducted at the gene level using bulk RNA-sequencing, which does not resolve cell-type heterogeneity within the smooth muscle cell preparations and may obscure subpopulation-specific transcriptional responses.
