## Executive Summary

Differential gene expression analysis of the *airway* dataset was performed to characterize transcriptomic differences between dexamethasone-treated and untreated human airway smooth muscle cells (*Homo sapiens*). Across 16,596 genes tested, 4,099 were identified as statistically significant differentially expressed genes (DEGs) at a false discovery rate threshold corresponding to adjusted p-values from DESeq2, representing a substantial proportion of the transcriptome responsive to treatment. Of these, 2,201 genes were upregulated and 1,898 were downregulated in the treated condition relative to the untreated control. Principal component analysis revealed that the first two principal components accounted for 48% and 24% of total variance, respectively, suggesting that treatment condition was the dominant source of transcriptional variation in the dataset and that the experimental groups were well separated in reduced-dimensional space.

## Results Interpretation

Among the most statistically significant DEGs, several genes with established functional relevance to glucocorticoid signaling and cellular stress responses were prominent. *SPARCL1* (log₂FC = 4.57, padj = 3.58 × 10⁻¹³²), a secreted matricellular protein involved in cell adhesion and extracellular matrix remodeling, exhibited the largest effect size among the top-ranked genes and was positively associated with treatment. *SAMHD1* (log₂FC = 3.76, padj = 1.88 × 10⁻¹²⁴), a dNTPase with known roles in innate immune regulation and cell cycle control, and *MAOA* (log₂FC = 3.35, padj = 2.57 × 10⁻¹¹⁹), encoding monoamine oxidase A, which is involved in the catabolism of biogenic amines and has previously been reported as a glucocorticoid-responsive gene, were also strongly upregulated. *DUSP1* (log₂FC = 2.95, padj = 1.22 × 10⁻¹²⁴), a dual-specificity phosphatase that negatively regulates MAP kinase signaling and is a well-characterized primary glucocorticoid response gene, and *CACNB2* (log₂FC = 3.29, padj = 8.60 × 10⁻¹³²), a voltage-gated calcium channel auxiliary subunit, were similarly among the most significantly upregulated transcripts. It should be noted that while effect sizes for these genes were large, their biological importance requires further functional validation beyond statistical significance alone.

Pathway enrichment analysis across 415 significant terms provided additional context for the transcriptional landscape associated with treatment. Upregulated genes were enriched for Gene Ontology Biological Process (GO:BP) terms related to developmental processes, anatomical structure development, and response to stimulus, consistent with glucocorticoid-mediated transcriptional reprogramming that engages developmental and adaptive regulatory programs. Downregulated genes were enriched for terms encompassing cell communication, signaling, and the regulation of multicellular organismal processes, suggesting that treatment was associated with suppression of intercellular signaling networks, which aligns with the known anti-inflammatory and immunosuppressive mechanisms of glucocorticoid action. The predominance of GO:BP terms (314 of 415 total enriched terms) relative to KEGG (9) and Reactome (6) annotations indicates that the functional signal was most broadly captured at the level of biological process ontology, though the convergence across multiple databases lends greater confidence to the observed pathway-level patterns.

## Limitations

Several methodological and biological limitations should be considered when interpreting these findings. As an observational transcriptomic study, the analysis identifies gene expression changes statistically associated with treatment but cannot establish causal directionality or mechanistic sufficiency for any individual gene or pathway. The *airway* dataset is derived from a controlled in vitro cell culture system, which, while enabling experimental rigor, may not fully recapitulate the complexity of in vivo airway physiology; therefore, generalizability of these findings to clinical contexts should be approached with caution. Furthermore, statistical significance, as captured by adjusted p-values, does not inherently equate to biological relevance, and the large number of DEGs identified (4,099) underscores the need for orthogonal validation approaches—such as quantitative PCR, protein-level assays, or functional perturbation experiments—to prioritize candidates of genuine mechanistic interest. Finally, pathway enrichment terms such as "developmental process" and "multicellular organismal process" are inherently broad and may reflect gene set composition artifacts rather than specific, coordinated biological programs; interpretation of these higher-order annotations should therefore be made conservatively and in conjunction with more granular pathway resources.
